üéØ Opening Conversation Framework

STEP 1: Problem Identification

You: "Tell me about the biggest leadership challenge you're facing in your organization right now."

Listen for: Communication issues, team dysfunction, unclear expectations, performance gaps

STEP 2: Impact Exploration

You: "How is this leadership challenge impacting your bottom line? What's it costing you in terms of productivity, turnover, or missed opportunities?"

Listen for: Specific dollar amounts, time wasted, lost revenue, recruitment costs

STEP 3: Current Solution Inquiry

You: "What have you tried so far to address these leadership issues?"

Listen for: Generic training, one-size-fits-all solutions, lack of measurement

You: "I work with a science-backed leadership assessment called TPEAINDEX that measures effectiveness across 6 critical leadership dimensions. It gives you concrete data about where your leaders excel and exactly where to focus development efforts."

Credibility Statement

You: "This isn't another personality test - it's used by Fortune 500 companies and has a 95% client satisfaction rate. The assessment takes 10 minutes but provides insights that transform leadership teams."

üîç Discovery Questions

  • Current State: "How do you currently measure leadership effectiveness?"
  • Pain Points: "What happens when leaders don't communicate effectively with their teams?"
  • Decision Making: "Who else would be involved in deciding on a leadership development initiative?"
  • Budget Reality: "What have you invested in leadership development in the past year?"
  • Timeline: "How quickly do you need to see improvements in leadership performance?"
  • Success Metrics: "What would success look like if we improved leadership effectiveness by 20-30%?"
  • Urgency: "What's the cost of waiting another 6 months to address these leadership issues?"

üõ°Ô∏è Common Objections & Responses

OBJECTION: "We need to think about it."

Response: "I understand. What specific concerns do you have that we should address? Is it the methodology, the investment, or the implementation process?"

Follow-up: "What would need to happen for you to feel confident moving forward?"

OBJECTION: "We don't have budget right now."

Response: "I appreciate your honesty about budget. Help me understand - if poor leadership is costing you $50K annually in turnover and lost productivity, and this assessment costs less than $1,400, when would the budget conversation make sense?"

OBJECTION: "We've tried assessments before and they didn't work."

Response: "That's exactly why I love working with organizations like yours. Most assessments tell you personality type but not leadership effectiveness. TPEAINDEX measures actual leadership behaviors that drive results. What specifically didn't work with your previous assessments?"

OBJECTION: "Our leaders don't have time for this."

Response: "I hear this a lot, and it's actually the best indicator that you need this assessment. When leaders are too busy to invest 10 minutes in their own development, that's usually a sign of ineffective leadership practices. The assessment saves time by focusing development exactly where it's needed."

OBJECTION: "Can you send me more information?"

Response: "Absolutely. I want to send you the most relevant information. Help me understand - are you evaluating this for yourself as a leader, or for your entire leadership team? And what's your biggest concern about implementing something like this?"
